Renato Berlinghieri

PhD Student

MIT, LIDS

Welcome to my personal page! I am a 5th year PhD student at MIT working in LIDS with Tamara Broderick. I am broadly interested in AI4Science, in particular generative models and uncertainty quantification for biological and environmental applications. More precisely, I develop probabilistic machine learning methods for spatiotemporal data that embed physical and biological structure into their design. During my PhD, I closely collaborated with domain scientists, including oceanographers, biologists, and atmospheric chemists.

This past summer I interned in the Center for Computational Mathematics at the Flatiron Institute in New York City where I worked on Simulation-based Inference with Diana Cai. I previously interned as a Machine Learning researcher in the Health AI team at Apple. Before joining MIT, I completed a MSc in Data Science at Bocconi University advised by Igor Pr√ºnster. Prior to that, I obtained a BSc in Economics and Computer Science advised by Massimo Marinacci.

Besides research, I have always been very passionate about having an active role in the communities I am part of. I have previously served as co-President of the MIT EECS Graduate Students’ Association. I am currently serving as Program Chair and organizer of the Bayesian Decision Making and Uncertainty workshop at NeurIPS and organizer of the Virtual Seminar Series on Gaussian Processes, Spatiotemporal Modeling, and Decision-making Systems.
